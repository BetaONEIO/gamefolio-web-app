We at Prayer for Israel were deeply saddened to hear of the passing of Pastor John Angliss. John was a dear friend of the ministry and was present from the very early days of PFI’s history. Over many years, he faithfully attended and spoke at numerous Prayer for Israel gatherings, carrying a genuine love for the Jewish people and the nation of Israel.

John was born in Caversham, Reading in 1945 into a God-fearing family and came to faith around 1962 through Caversham Baptist Church and the influence of Youth for Christ Bible rallies. Following a deep experience of the Holy Spirit in 1965, he sensed the Lord’s call towards ministry and began preaching in July 1967. He married his wife Mary in 1971, and together they stepped into full-time faith ministry in 1974.

In January 1977, John became Pastor of Mitford Mission Hall, which later became South of Reading Christian Fellowship in 1985. Under his leadership, the fellowship grew from a small gathering into a nationally recognised independent full gospel church. John faithfully shepherded the fellowship for 40 years before stepping into a wider itinerant ministry through AIM (Ambassador International Ministry) in 2017.

Throughout his ministry, John travelled extensively, speaking at churches, conferences, Bible weeks, and gatherings across the UK, Ireland, Israel, India, France, Tanzania, Sri Lanka, and beyond. He also served in leadership roles with several ministries, including Derek Prince Ministries-UK, Focus on Israel, and Prayer for Israel, where he made a lasting impact through his teaching, support, writing, and encouragement.

John carried a deep burden for the Church to walk in both the truth of God’s Word and the leading of the Holy Spirit. One of his greatest passions was teaching on Israel and end-times prophecy, subjects he approached with conviction, wisdom, and faithfulness to Scripture.

Many of the PFI staff, volunteers, and prayer partners knew John personally and can testify to his kind, gentle, and loving character. He was a man who carried wisdom, humility, and a deep devotion to the Lord. His life and ministry touched many, and his encouragement to stand with Israel and remain faithful to God’s purposes will not be forgotten.

While we grieve his passing, we rejoice in the assurance that John is now in the presence of the Lord whom he faithfully served for so many years. John, you will be greatly missed, but our loss is only temporary, for we look forward to being reunited together in eternity.

Our love and prayers are with Mary, the family, and all who knew and loved him at this time.

“Precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of His saints.” – Psalm 116:15
